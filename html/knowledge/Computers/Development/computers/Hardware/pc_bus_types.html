Bus Types

ISA

Industry Standard Architechture. A 16-bit slot, this is the oldest of the three bus types. It is typically still found in nearly every computer sold today.Generally used for modems and most sound cards.

VLB

VESA Local Bus. A 32-bit slot, this originated in and was designed for 486 systems Generally used by video cards and hard drive controllers Cards in these slots are not plug-n-play.

PCI

Peripheral Component Interface. Also a 32-bit slot, is mainly found in Pentium Class systems, but also can be found on some 486 systems. Generally used by IDE/SCSI controllers, video cards, network cards, video capture boards, and some sound cards.

AGP

Accelerated Graphics Port. This newest bus was designed to be used exclusively by the display adapter. Currently, only Intel's 440LX chipset (found in Pentium II motherboards) supports AGP. Upon the release of next generation operating systems (Windows98, Windows NT 5.0), the performance of AGP will increase at its importance will be quite obvious.
