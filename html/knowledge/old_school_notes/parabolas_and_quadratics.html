A parabola is the set of all points in the plane equidistant from a given line L (the conic section directrix) and a given point F not on the line (the focus). The focal parameter (i.e., the distance between the directrix and focus) is therefore given by p=2a, where a is the distance from the vertex to the directrix or focus.

The equation of a parabola (in cartesian coordinates) can be given by y = ax^2 + bx + c or x = ay^2 + by + c

In the form of the equation y = ax^2 + bx + c
 if a is positive the parabola will open up
if a is negative the parabola will open down

The x coordinate of the vertex is given by the formula x = - {b \over 2a }
