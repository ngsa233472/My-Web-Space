Geometric Series

\begin{align}{\sum^\infty_{k=1}}{1 \over k} = 1 + {1 \over 2} + {1 \over 3} + {1 \over 4} + {1 \over 5} + \cdots\end{align}

This series diverges P Series
\begin{align}{\sum^\infty_{k=1}}{1 \over {k^p}} = 1 + {1 \over 2^p} + {1 \over 3^p} + {1 \over 4^p} + {1 \over 5^p} \cdots\end{align} Converges if \begin{align}{ p \gt 1 }\end{align} and diverges if \begin{align}0 \lt p \lt 1 \end{align} Alternating Series
\begin{align}{\sum^\infty_{k=1}}{(-1)^k}a_k = -1a_0 + 1a_1 - 1a_2 \cdots\end{align} \begin{align}{\sum^\infty_{k=1}}{(-1)^{k+1}}a_k = 1a_0 - 1a_1 + 1a_2 \cdots\end{align}
Coverges if
(1) \begin{align}a_1 \geq a_2 \geq a_3 \geq a_4 \geq \cdots \geq a_k \lt 0 \end{align} (2) \begin{align}{lim \over {k \rightarrow + \infty}} a_k = 0\end{align} Power Series
\begin{align}{\sum^\infty_{k=0}}c_kx^k = c_0 + c_1x +c_2x^2 + \cdots + c_kx^k\end{align}
Divergence for power series has one of three conditions.

(1) The series converges only for x=0
(2) The series converges absolutely for all x
(3) The series converges absolutely for all x in some finite open interval
