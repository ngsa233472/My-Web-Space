Vectors and Scalars

Definition:

A scalar is a quantity like mass or temperature that only has a magnitude. On the other had, a vector is a mathematical object that has magnitude and direction.

Typical notation to designate a vector is a boldfaced character, a character with and arrow on it, or a character with a line under it

B , \begin{align}\buildrel\rightarrow\over B or B

Vectors can be expressed in many coordinate systems. to simplify this discussion we will limit the vectors to two dimensions and only discuss polar cartesian and polar coordinate systems.
